Roman Mythology

Mythological Figures

Jupiter

King of the gods, ruler of the sky and thunder.

Juno

Queen of the gods, protector of marriage and women.

Neptune

God of the sea, earthquakes, and horses.

Minerva

Goddess of wisdom, crafts, and strategic warfare.

Apollo

God of music, poetry, light, and healing.

Diana

Goddess of the hunt, wilderness, wild animals, and the Moon.

Mars

God of war and agriculture.

Venus

Goddess of love, beauty, desire, and fertility.

Mercury

Messenger of the gods, god of commerce, eloquence, and travel.

Ceres

Goddess of agriculture, grain crops, fertility, and motherly relationships.

Vulcan

God of fire, metalworking, and crafts.

Bacchus

God of wine, fertility, theatre, and religious ecstasy.

Festivals

Saturnalia

A festival in honor of Saturn, marked by feasting, gift-giving, and role reversals. Origin: Rome

Lupercalia

An ancient pastoral festival, possibly related to the founding of Rome. Origin: Rome

Floralia

A festival in honor of Flora, the goddess of flowers and spring. Origin: Rome

Parentalia

A festival to honor deceased ancestors. Origin: Rome
